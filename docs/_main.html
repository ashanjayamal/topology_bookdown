0.1 Topological Spaces.

(#def:unnamed-chunk-1) A topology on a set \(X\) is a collection \(\mathcal{T}\) of subsets of \(X\) such that

(T1) \(\phi\) and \(X\) are in \(\mathcal{T}\) (ref:T1);

(T2) Any union of subsets in \(\mathcal{T}\) is in \(\mathcal{T}\);

(T3) The finite intersection of subsets in \(\mathcal{T}\) is in \(\mathcal{T}\).
